## I. VOICE CALIBRATION GUIDELINES

### Core Voice DNA: "McKinsey meets Harvard Business Review"

**PRIMARY VOICE ATTRIBUTES:**

1. **Executive Authority** — speak peer-to-peer with C-suite leaders
   - Use first-person organizational anthropology perspective ("My research reveals...")
   - Reference strategic frameworks familiar to executives (BCG Matrix, Porter's Five Forces, Blue Ocean)
   - Quantify impact in business metrics (market cap loss, EBITDA impact, strategic value erosion)

2. **Academic Rigor** — maintain scholarly credibility
   - Evidence-driven conclusions with banking-level verification
   - Primary source documentation (SEC filings, earnings reports, court proceedings)
   - Methodological transparency in investigation approach

3. **Strategic Accessibility** — complex ideas in executive-digestible format
   - Clear cause-and-effect chains linking organizational behavior to business outcomes
   - Actionable insights with implementation frameworks
   - Time-efficient consumption (executives read for decision-making, not entertainment)

### VOICE EVOLUTION FRAMEWORK

**ELIMINATE (Satirical Elements):**
- Pop culture references for humor value
- Sarcastic commentary on corporate dysfunction
- Entertainment-driven narrative choices
- "Clever" wordplay that doesn't advance strategic understanding

**PRESERVE (Academic Authority):**
- Literary archetypes as analytical frameworks
- Historical case studies as pattern recognition tools
- Rigorous source verification and citation standards
- Investigative methodology transparency

**ENHANCE (Executive Resonance):**
- Strategic implications clearly articulated
- ROI/business impact quantification
- Implementation readiness assessment
- Risk mitigation frameworks

## II. ACADEMIC-SATIRICAL BALANCE

### APPROPRIATE HUMOR CONTEXTS

**WHERE SATIRICAL ELEMENTS ARE STRATEGIC:**

1. **Pattern Recognition** — irony highlighting systematic failures
   - "Target Canada's 124 'functioning' stores with empty shelves exemplifies the metrics-reality gap plaguing modern organizations"
   - NOT: "Corporate dashboards showing green while Rome burns — peak corporate comedy"

2. **Memorable Frameworks** — wit serving pedagogical purpose
   - "The Sherlock Holmes method: observe what competitors miss"
   - NOT: "Elementary, my dear Watson — another agile transformation disaster"

3. **Cognitive Reframing** — humor as insight catalyst
   - "Conway's Law as corporate forensics: organizational structure leaves fingerprints on every product"
   - NOT: "Conway called it — your org chart is showing in your API mess"

### ELIMINATION CRITERIA

**Remove satirical elements that:**
- Mock without advancing understanding
- Target specific companies/individuals for entertainment
- Prioritize cleverness over strategic value
- Distract from analytical rigor
